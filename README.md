## Project Summary
This is an end-to-end **Data Analytics project** focused on analyzing quick commerce grocery sales data to identify revenue drivers, customer behavior, and outlet performance. The project demonstrates hands-on experience with **Excel, SQL (MySQL), Power BI, and Python**, following a complete analytics lifecycle from raw data to business insights.

---

## Business Problem
Quick commerce platforms generate large volumes of transactional data but require structured analysis to:
- Monitor sales performance and revenue trends  
- Identify high-performing products and outlets  
- Understand customer preferences using ratings and order behavior  
- Enable data-driven decision-making  

---

## Objectives
- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Define and calculate key business KPIs  
- Conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
- Build interactive dashboards and reports  
- Generate actionable business insights  

---

## Dataset Overview
- Domain: Retail / Quick Commerce / Grocery Analytics  
- Data Level: Item-level transactional data  
- Formats: Excel and CSV  
- Key Fields: Item Type, Item Fat Content, Outlet Size, Outlet Location Type, Outlet Establishment Year, Sales Revenue, Customer Rating  

A single dataset is reused across all tools to maintain consistency and reproducibility.

---

## Tools & Skills Used
- **Excel:** Data cleaning, KPI calculations, Pivot Tables, slicers, dashboard creation  
- **SQL (MySQL):** Data cleaning, CASE statements, aggregations, GROUP BY queries, KPI analysis  
- **Power BI:** DAX measures, interactive dashboards, data modeling, visualization  
- **Python:** Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
- **Core Skills:** Data analysis, data transformation, business analytics, data visualization  

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Total Revenue  
- Average Revenue per Order  
- Total Orders  
- Average Customer Rating  

---

## Analysis Performed
- Product category revenue analysis  
- Sales comparison by fat content  
- Outlet performance analysis by size, location, and type  
- Revenue trend analysis based on outlet establishment year  
- Cross-validation of insights using SQL, Excel, Power BI, and Python  

---

## Key Business Insights
- Medium-sized outlets contribute the highest share of total revenue  
- Tier-3 outlet locations outperform Tier-1 and Tier-2 in overall sales  
- Regular fat products generate higher revenue, while low-fat products maintain steady demand  
- Older outlets show more stable and consistent revenue patterns
